Why I built this

I use 96-well plates regularly in a plate reader — dose-response assays, growth inhibition curves, dye uptake experiments. Every time I sit down to design a new experiment, I end up doing the same mental gymnastics: how much stock do I need per well? how many wells do I have for this replicate count? does this volume even pipette reliably?

I was doing it in spreadsheets, then on paper, then in my head while already in the lab — and making small mistakes that cost me an afternoon re-running controls. I wanted something I could pull up on any computer in the lab, type in my numbers, and get a plate map and bench protocol back immediately.

I also change things constantly. A colleague asks to borrow a row for their control. My stock concentration changes. I want to add a replicate. I needed a tool that lets me adjust things on the fly and see the effects instantly — without re-doing a spreadsheet from scratch.

Dilution Designer was built for exactly this: fast, flexible experiment planning for anyone who works with 96-well plates. No account, no subscription, no install — just open the page and start planning.

What it does

⚗️

Volume calculations

Enter your stock concentration and target concentrations — the tool calculates exactly how much compound and culture to add to each well, with pipettability warnings for tricky volumes.

🗺️

Live plate map

See your experiment on a full 96-well plate in real time. Colors, replicate labels, and concentration values update instantly as you change inputs.

🎨

Fully customizable colors

Click any condition swatch or control dot to open a color picker. Choose from a palette or enter a custom hex code — the plate map updates live.

🚫

Exclude wells, rows, or columns

Click the row/column buttons to exclude entire rows or columns. Or click and drag directly on the plate to exclude individual wells — concentrations automatically redistribute.

📐

6 fill order modes

By column (top→bottom or bottom→top), by row (left→right or right→left), snake/serpentine, or spread (replicates distributed evenly across the plate).

🧫

Controls & blanks

Toggle negative controls, media blanks, culture blanks, and positive controls — each with their own replicate count and color. Add custom controls for any use case.

📋

Master mix table

Automatically calculates master mix volumes with 10% overage so you don't run out mid-experiment.

📤

Export options

Download a CSV with all per-well volumes and master mix data, copy a plain-text summary to clipboard, print the full protocol, or print just the plate map.

🧪

Intermediate stock support

If your stock is too concentrated to pipette directly, switch to intermediate stock mode and get a recipe for making the right dilution first.

Who it's for

Dilution Designer works for any assay that uses a 96-well plate. Some examples of where it's useful:

  • Dose-response / IC50 assays — antibiotics, small molecules, enzymes, toxins
  • Growth inhibition assays — MIC determination, antifungals, phage
  • Dye uptake assays — crystal violet, propidium iodide, fluorescent dyes
  • Reporter assays — GFP/luciferase induction curves with inducers like IPTG or doxycycline
  • Enzyme kinetics — substrate titration, inhibitor dose-response
  • Cell viability assays — MTT, alamarBlue, resazurin
  • Any experiment where you're testing a compound at multiple concentrations with replicates

How to use it

  1. Enter your compound name — first field in the sidebar. This appears throughout the protocol and exports.
  2. Set your stock concentration and culture volume per well — the math is based on these.
  3. Add target concentrations — type them one by one, or use a preset dilution series (2×, log-ish, linear).
  4. Configure replicates and fill order — choose how many replicates per condition and how the plate fills.
  5. Exclude any wells, rows, or columns you don't want to use — click row/column buttons or drag on the plate map.
  6. Toggle controls as needed — negative control, blanks, positive control, or add a custom one.
  7. Export — download the CSV, print the full protocol, or print just the plate map to take to the bench.
Tip: everything updates live. Change your stock concentration, add a condition, switch fill direction — the plate map, volume table, and bench instructions all update instantly. You don't need to "submit" anything.
