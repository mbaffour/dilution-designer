Why I built this

I use 96-well plates regularly in a plate reader — dose-response assays, growth inhibition curves, dye uptake experiments, phage infection assays. Every time I sit down to design a new experiment, I end up doing the same mental gymnastics: how much stock do I need per well? how many wells do I have for this replicate count? does this volume even pipette reliably?

I was doing it in spreadsheets, then on paper, then in my head while already in the lab — and making small mistakes that cost me an afternoon re-running controls. I wanted something I could pull up on any computer in the lab, type in my numbers, and get a plate map and bench protocol back immediately.

I also change things constantly. A colleague asks to borrow a row for their control. My stock concentration changes. I want to add a replicate. I want to design a phage infection with different MOIs across the same plate. I needed a tool that lets me adjust things on the fly and see the effects instantly — without re-doing a spreadsheet from scratch.

Dilution Designer was built for exactly this: fast, flexible experiment planning for anyone who works with 96-well plates. No account, no subscription, no install — just open the page and start planning.

Two experiment modes

⚗️

Plasmid / Induction Mode

For concentration-gradient experiments: antibiotics, inducers (IPTG, doxycycline), small molecules, dyes, enzymes.

  • Set stock concentration and target concentrations
  • Volumes calculated per well (compound + culture)
  • Master mix table with 10% overage
  • Intermediate stock support for high concentrations
  • Pipettability warnings for tricky volumes
🦠

Phage Infection Mode

For MOI-based infection assays: phage dose-response, host range testing, plaque formation, infection efficiency.

  • Enter bacterial concentration and phage titer
  • Set target MOI per condition
  • Exact phage volume per well calculated automatically
  • Built-in MOI calculator for quick single-well checks
  • Serial dilution planner for prep from stock

What it does

🎨

Interactive plate painting

Four tools: Paint (assign a condition to any well), Replicate (drag horizontally or vertically to fill a run of wells with the same condition), Erase, and Exclude. Click or drag directly on the plate map.

🧬

Gen5-style plate layout

Large square wells (inspired by Biotek Gen5 software), well IDs visible in each cell (A1–H12), clear condition color fill. Each replicate is shown as its own independent well — never summed or collapsed.

🔬

MOI calculator

Standalone calculator in the sidebar: enter bacterial concentration (CFU/mL), phage titer (PFU/mL), desired MOI, and infection volume — get the exact phage volume to add in µL.

💉

Serial dilution planner

Plan a dilution series from stock to working titer. Set your minimum pipettable volume — the planner enforces it at every step and outputs a complete recipe with transfer and diluent volumes.

🌙

Three UI themes

Day (bright, high-contrast), Night (dark mode), and Ambient (warm, low-contrast — easy on the eyes during long bench sessions). Toggle in the header.

🎨

Fully customizable colors

Click any condition swatch or control dot to open a color picker. Choose from a quick palette or enter a custom hex code — the plate map updates live.

🚫

Exclude wells, rows, or columns

Click row/column buttons to exclude entire rows or columns. Or use the Exclude tool and drag directly on the plate to mark individual wells as off-limits. Excluded wells show a striped pattern and are omitted from all calculations.

🧫

Controls & blanks

Toggle negative controls, media blanks, culture blanks, and positive controls — each with their own color. Add custom controls for any use case.

📋

Master mix table

Automatically calculates master mix volumes with 10% overage so you don't run out mid-experiment. Groups by condition and lists all well IDs in each group.

📤

Export options

Download a minimal CSV (plate map + per-well volumes — no extra clutter), copy a plain-text summary, print the full protocol, or print just the plate map to take to the bench.

🧪

Intermediate stock support

If your stock is too concentrated to pipette directly, switch to intermediate stock mode and get a recipe for making the right dilution first.

🏷️

Compound / reagent name

Name your compound or reagent — it appears throughout the protocol, legend, bench instructions, and CSV header so your exports are self-documenting.

Who it's for

Dilution Designer works for any assay that uses a 96-well plate. Some examples of where it's useful:

  • Dose-response / IC50 assays — antibiotics, small molecules, enzymes, toxins
  • Growth inhibition assays — MIC determination, antifungals, phage
  • Phage infection assays — MOI dose-response, host range, infection efficiency
  • Dye uptake assays — crystal violet, propidium iodide, fluorescent dyes
  • Reporter assays — GFP/luciferase induction curves with IPTG or doxycycline
  • Enzyme kinetics — substrate titration, inhibitor dose-response
  • Cell viability assays — MTT, alamarBlue, resazurin
  • Any experiment where you're testing conditions at multiple levels with replicates

How to use it

Plasmid / induction experiments

  1. Select Plasmid mode from the mode toggle at the top of the sidebar.
  2. Enter your compound name — appears throughout the protocol and exports.
  3. Set your stock concentration and culture volume per well.
  4. Add conditions — type target concentrations. Each appears in the palette.
  5. Paint the plate — select a condition from the palette, pick the Paint or Replicate tool, and click/drag wells to assign them.
  6. Toggle controls as needed — negative control, blanks, positive control, or add a custom one.
  7. Export — download the CSV, print the full protocol, or print the plate map.

Phage infection experiments

  1. Select Phage mode from the mode toggle.
  2. Enter your bacterial concentration (CFU/mL), phage titer (PFU/mL), and infection volume per well (µL).
  3. Add MOI conditions — each condition represents a target MOI. The phage volume to add is calculated automatically.
  4. Use the serial dilution planner if you need to prepare working titer from a concentrated stock — set your minimum pipettable volume and get a step-by-step recipe.
  5. Paint the plate and export — same as Plasmid mode.
Tip: everything updates live. Change your stock concentration, repaint a well, switch modes, toggle a theme — the plate map, volume table, and bench instructions all update instantly. You don't need to "submit" anything.
